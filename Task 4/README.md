🏥 Task 4: Predicting Insurance Claim Amounts
The Task Objective: Formulate a continuous regression model to accurately estimate medical insurance premium charges utilizing personal health and demographic metadata.

Cleaned and encoded binary categories (smoker, sex) into mathematical indicators. Plotted localized feature correlations and visually mapped interactions of age, BMI, and smoking history against costs. Trained a Linear Regression framework and evaluated prediction error using Mean Absolute Error (MAE) and Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE).

Results and Insights: Smoking status paired with a BMI exceeding 30 triggers a massive, non-linear compounding spike in medical charges. Age shows a steady, linear baseline upward trend in baseline insurance premiums across all demographics.
